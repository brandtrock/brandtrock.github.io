My UI Dev Process

Introduction

This static, single-page site was created to help provide some insight into how I personally take on user interface/front-end tasks. Being short on time, I kept this simple.

Please scroll through this page to follow through my process and let me know what questions and concerns you may have when it comes to my take on UI dev.

Thanks!

Step 1: Content Creation

I started by writing down the goal of this webpage: Goal - To provide insight into how I do UI development; my personal process. I used this as the pinpoint to brainstorming the content I wanted to share on the page.

Initially, I was not sure what I was going to do and I figured that I would create something and use "Lorem Ipsum" text everywhere; however, I was not sold on that approach.

After talking with my wife an idea popped into my mind - I should make the page about the process of making the page.

Web-ception? A page about building that page!

Step 2: Wireframing

How should I lay this page out? There is not much to it, but the continuous reading of the content is most important.

Traditional Design?
Modern Design?

Using a modern design fits the overall narration of the goal of the page better than a traditional design (header, left nav, content on the right).

Bonus: The modern design scales easier on different screens.

Step 3: Prototyping

Now that I had a clear direction, I was able to make a better decision on what technology to use.

As always, at the root of the page are my favorite tools: HTML, CSS, JavaScript (specifically jQuery). I also used Bootstrap to provide the responsive approach and a good, solid foundation to build off of.

After deciding to keep the design, minimal and simple and the tech to use, I started building out this page.

I used GitHub Pages!

Summary

To sum up how I work, here is an overview of the bolded points throughout this page.

A clear understanding of the goal is the first step. Brainstorming helps to focus on how to achieve that specific goal at a high-level, which helps a team communicate to ensure that everyone understands the problem.

The content of any application or tool is the most important part of that product. That information needs to be clear, easy-to-use, and understand.

The development of an application or tool must fulfill the intended goal through simple design based on a clear direction established by all team members involved in the process.
